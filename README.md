# Feature explanation

### Used features

**Numbeo is good**. But only to compare private services.

**Numbeo purchasing power index** does not evaluate the cost of school, university, and kinder
garden with respect to the ratio of free public institutions available. And doesn't consider
the unemployment rate.

**Numbeo property to income index** does not account for home ownership rate. 
Ex-social block countries look much better that way. 

**Coefficients in numbeo quality of life index** doesn't reflect my view of an image too.

**Median age** feature is constructed by subtraction 30 from country median age. It is a controversial
method. This method reflects my personal consideration of an ideal median age of a country. Too young
societies tend to have too many changes, too old too little. Anyway, it is just my way of seeing
things  

### Rejected features

**Median Wealth per adult by Credit Suisse**  3K $ for Russia looks really awkward. 
Because 86% of adults leave in their own homes. A lot of households have two and more.
Some people have land in possession. Those should give roughly around 10K without consideration of cars,
stocks, banking accounts, cash, etc.

**Unemployment rate**
I live in Russia, thus having Russian optics to observe the world. In Russia, the unemployment rate 
is counted on labor exchange users only. That's why the unemployment ratio is so small in Russia.
